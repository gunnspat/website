Q. Is PAT Testing law?
A. YES! Electrical regulations currently in force in the UK put a responsibility of "due care" on the duty holder i.e. Employer, landlord etc to ensure that their electrical appliances are safe. PAT testing is an effective way to ensure that you comply with those regulations.

Q. What equipment is covered?
A. Generally speaking, all equipment fitted with a standard BS1363 plug is covered. This includes PCs, kettles, irons, televisions, toasters, microwaves, power tools, washing machines, vacuum cleaners etc. More specifically, The National Association of Professional Inspectors and Testers (napit) define a portable appliance as 'any electrical item which can or is intended, to be moved whilst connected to an electrical supply.'

Q. Do I really need all those stickers plastered all over my lovely new equipment?
A. Yes! All equipment needs to be labelled to ensure it can be uniquely identified in the test results. However, at Gunn’s Portable Appliance Testing we only use the highest quality, discreet labels, applied to the mains cable wherever possible, plus we remove your old labels wherever possible!

Q. Does my equipment need to be switched off for the test?
A. Yes! It is impossible to carry out a PAT test with the unit switched on. The test invariably involves a thorough inspection of the mains plug and fuse, which is impossible to do without switching the unit off this is why Gunn’s Portable Appliance Testing offers an out of hours testing at no extra cost. This is an ideal solution for your business as IT equipment may need to stay on during office hours.
Q. How long will testing take?
A. This depends on the type of equipment and the layout of your site amongst other factors. Around 16 tests per hour is the average.
Q. Could the test damage my equipment?
A. Potentially, Yes! Modern test equipment is capable of testing at high currents and voltages. Applying the wrong test to your equipment could cause irreparable damage. That is why all Gunn’s Portable Appliance Testing engineers are fully qualified to the relevant City & Guilds 2377 standards and are covered by public liability insurance.
Q. What will I receive after he testing?
A. Piece of mind! Also you will receive confirmation of testing on the day of inspection. All test results will be recorded and delivered via E-mail normally within 24 hours of testing and your testing certificate will be posted to you.
Q. When and how do I pay for my PAT Testing?
A. Payment is normally made on day of inspection via cash or cheque; our engineers will supply you with an receipt of any payments made and invoice will be supplied with your testing certificate via post, cheques should be made payable to ‘Gunn’s Portable Appliance Testing’.
For BACS payments you are required to pay the flat rate upfront, A pro-forma invoice can be provided upon request. Any additional items will be invoiced separately supplied with certificate, payment for additional items will be due within 14 days of invoice date.
